CAP and your Application

We’ll cover the CAP theorem and how it applies to modern networked databases. What do Consistency, Availability and Partition tolerance mean to your application design? How do solutions like Cassandra, Postgres or Mongo prioritize these concerns and why might you choose one over the other?

Finally, how does CAP(Brewer’s theorem) apply to users of your app? If you have more that one web server you are a distributed system, and subject to CAP as well.

Matt Overstreet

Matt Overstreet spent many a Saturday begging his Dad to help him compile Unix games from the VAX at Virginia Tech. Since then he took some time off to learn about humans, including work on a Bachelor of Fine Arts in Acting and Directing. Tired of the crazy hour and egos in the entertainment industry he returned to the family business as a bit cobbler. Starting out fixing printers for call centers he decided computers should fix themselves (and filed a patent or two around that idea). Fortunately, he had a network of 20k+ nodes with which to tinker. Next came small business web development. Then, full circle to lead architect for Cambio.com, the Jonas Groups online content network, TomBrady.com and AliStories.com.

He has retired from entertainment a second time and is now working on navigating search and big data. He has come to realize that Theatre and Technology are the same; what happens on stage is irrelevant, what happens in the audience is key.
