Semantic Web

Where is it^H^H^H^H^H When Did It Get Here?

Q: When will the Semantic Web get here?
A: What makes you think it isn't already here?

I will introduce a variety of tools and standards from the Semantic Web technology stack. These are becomingly increasingly widely used on the Web and in organizations of all shapes and sizes. They are applicable to every technology and will be key to eliminating data silos of all forms. These ideas build upon trends that have become core to our IT strategies and it is no great leap to see how they will impact our lives even more than the Web already has. As analytics, machine learning and Big Data become important to us, these technologies will amplify their impact more than they can on their own.

Come see how Webs of Data are important to understand, not in the future, but today.

Brian Sletten

Brian Sletten is a liberal arts-educated software engineer with a focus on forward-leaning technologies. His experience has spanned many industries including retail, banking, online games, defense, finance, hospitality and health care. He has a B.S. in Computer Science from the College of William and Mary and lives in Auburn, CA. He focuses on web architecture, resource-oriented computing, social networking, the Semantic Web, data science, scalable systems, security consulting and other technologies of the late 20th and early 21st Centuries. He is also a rabid reader, devoted foodie and has excellent taste in music. If pressed, he might tell you about his International Pop Recording career.

He is one of SemanticWeb.com's "Top 100 Semantic Web People".
